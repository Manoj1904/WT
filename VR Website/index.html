VR Hardware (Headsets)

The look of virtual reality (VR) is primarily defined by the hardware devices (VR headsets) and the virtual environments they present to users. Here's an overview of the key aspects that contribute to the look of VR:

Display Technology

VR headsets use high-resolution displays (often OLED or LCD) to provide sharp visuals. The resolution affects the clarity of virtual images and text within the VR environment.

Optics

Lenses in VR headsets are designed to provide a wide field of view (FOV), typically around 100-120 degrees, enhancing immersion by filling more of the user's peripheral vision.

Tracking and Sensors

Inside-out tracking systems use built-in sensors (cameras or infrared sensors) to track the position and movement of the headset and controllers.

Controls and Input

VR controllers vary in design but typically include triggers, buttons, and motion sensors for precise interaction with virtual objects and environments.

Why wow

Virtual Reality (VR) is used for a variety of purposes across different industries and fields due to its ability to create immersive, interactive, and realistic simulated environments.
